Courier Safety: How We Handle Fragile and High-Value Items

By Roebuck Courier Service

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Handling Fragile and High-Value Items

Not everything that needs couriering is robust. Glass laboratory equipment, precision engineering components, electronic prototypes, artwork, medical devices — these items need more than just fast delivery. They need careful handling from collection to drop-off.

The way a courier handles fragile and high-value items reveals a lot about their service quality. Here's how we approach it at Roebuck, and what you should expect from any professional courier service.

Why Fragile Items Are Vulnerable in Standard Delivery Networks

In a national parcel network, your package goes through multiple handling points: collection, local depot, regional hub, delivery depot, delivery vehicle. At each stage, it's picked up, put down, stacked, sorted, and loaded — often alongside hundreds of other packages of varying weights and sizes.

Even with "fragile" stickers, the reality is that automated sorting systems and busy depot workers don't always treat packages with the care they need. It's not malicious — it's a system designed for volume, not individual care.

A dedicated courier service removes all of these risks. Your fragile item travels alone, handled only by the driver who collects it and delivers it.

How We Handle Fragile Consignments

When you tell us you're sending something fragile, here's what happens:

Right vehicle selection. We'll choose a vehicle that gives your item the space it needs. A precision instrument shouldn't be wedged between other boxes in a packed van — it should have room to travel safely. Our fleet includes vehicles of various sizes to match the consignment.

Careful loading. Our drivers are experienced in loading fragile items securely. That means using the vehicle's anchor points, blankets, and straps where needed to prevent movement during transit.

Direct delivery. No depots, no sorting, no other packages to create pressure. Your item goes straight from A to B in a single, uninterrupted journey.

Smooth driving. It sounds obvious, but driving style matters. Aggressive braking, fast cornering, and speed bumps taken at speed can all damage fragile items. Professional courier drivers understand this.

Communication. If there's anything unusual about the delivery — a potholed access road, a loading bay with a step — the driver will take extra care and communicate any concerns.

Packaging Guidance for Fragile Items

Good courier handling starts with good packaging. Here are our recommendations:

  • Double box when possible. Place your item in its own box with internal padding, then put that box inside a larger outer box with cushioning material in the gap
  • Use appropriate padding. Bubble wrap, foam sheets, air pillows, or custom foam inserts depending on the item's fragility
  • Eliminate movement. The item shouldn't shift inside its packaging when the box is tilted or gently shaken
  • Mark clearly. Label the package as fragile with handling arrows showing the correct orientation
  • Consider custom packaging. For regularly shipped fragile items, investing in custom foam inserts or flight cases can pay for itself quickly in reduced damage

Our guide on preparing items for courier collection has more detail on packaging best practices.

Insurance for High-Value Items

Our standard goods-in-transit insurance covers your consignment during delivery. For particularly high-value items, we can discuss additional cover to give you complete peace of mind.

It's worth noting that many standard parcel services have surprisingly low compensation limits for damaged items — sometimes as little as £50. Make sure you know what cover is included before you send something valuable.

What We Regularly Handle

We regularly deliver fragile and high-value items across the East Midlands and UK, including:

  • Laboratory equipment and glass instruments
  • Precision engineering components and gauges
  • Medical devices and diagnostic equipment
  • Electronic prototypes and IT equipment
  • Artwork and framed items
  • Architectural models and samples
  • Optical and photographic equipment
